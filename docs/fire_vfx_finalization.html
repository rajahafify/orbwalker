Fire Combat VFX Finalization

Documented May 23, 2026. This captures the accepted Fire post-match VFX direction after tuning in the combat VFX gallery.

Accepted Direction

Fire is now finalized around three readable power bands:

  • Weak Fire, around 4 damage, should feel like a strong fireball spell without visually beating medium Fire.
  • Medium Fire should feel like a wildfire, with broader ember travel and a heavier hit.
  • Strong Fire should feel like a devastating meteor attack, screen-wide in atmosphere but enemy-heavy in impact.

Implementation Notes

  • Strong wide Fire no longer uses one stretched full-width flame or impact sheet. The previous lower yellow slab was replaced by fragmented impact clusters.
  • Top-tier Fire now combines meteor streaks, smaller overlapping impact bursts, embers, sparks, heat fog, and light instead of a single large rectangular texture.
  • Weak Fire impact minimums were reduced for replay and cast impact paths, including lower opacity, smaller pack layers, fewer spark/burst radii, and a smaller light radius.
  • Medium and strong Fire scaling were preserved after weak-tier tuning.
  • Damage labels remain above VFX with strong outline styling so large hits stay readable.

Debug Gallery

A dedicated VFX gallery was added for quick iteration on the real combat VFX paths.

  • res://scenes/debug/vfx_gallery_index.tscn lists available VFX entries by category.
  • res://scenes/debug/vfx_gallery_show.tscn plays full sequence, spool, travel, impact, and label phases.
  • The gallery supports amount presets such as 4, 12, 25, and 50 to compare tier scaling on one screen.

Validation

  • CombatModelTest.run_all() passed 15/15.
  • The debug VFX gallery scene launched headless without Godot parse/runtime errors.
