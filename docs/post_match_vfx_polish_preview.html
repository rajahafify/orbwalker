What Changed In-Game

The elemental damage replay now has three readable beats: mastery charge, visible launch travel, then the enemy impact. The isolated cards above show the impact texture target; the combat mock shows the timing pass.

Fire builds hot at the mastery, launches as a flame mass, then bursts on the enemy.
Ice gathers as cold breath and crystals, breezes forward, then shatters.
Earth rumbles under the mastery, slithers along the ground, then pulses with stone.
Healing rises upward with refreshing green streams and soft motes.
Armor creates a blue-white shield shell with blocking impact streaks.
Gold rewards rain coins down around the player target.

Implementation Notes

Replay Timing

Elemental damage now waits through a slower mastery cast sequence before applying enemy damage feedback: 0.86s charge, 0.82s travel, and 0.50s impact hold at normal combat speed.

VFX Presenter

CombatVfxPresenter owns the new mastery cast path, layered additive impact effects, result-number intensity scaling, and future-facing post-match VFX speed scaling.

Asset Registry

VisualRegistry now builds distinct procedural post-match textures for fire, ice, earth, gold, heart, armor, and damage, with aliases for healing and armor gain.

Element Language

Fire spools hot and launches as flame, ice gathers cold breath and shards, earth rumbles then slithers along the ground, healing rises, armor blocks, and gold rains coins.

Intensity Scaling

Higher result numbers increase tier, size, lifetime, brightness, particle count, and cast density while keeping board and target readability intact.

Validation

The change includes registry and speed-scale smoke coverage, a presenter spawn probe, combat model tests, and a Godot main-scene launch without runtime errors.
