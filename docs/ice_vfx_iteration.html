Ice Combat VFX Iteration

Documented May 23, 2026. This captures the current Ice post-match VFX direction after separating it from the generic elemental and beam-like paths.

Accepted Direction

Ice now has three readable power bands:

  • Weak Ice should feel like a compact ice ball spell with a crisp frost impact.
  • Medium Ice should feel like a windy ice block, with a chunky projectile core moving through snow and shard lanes.
  • Strong Ice should feel like a full blizzard, with screen-wide cold atmosphere focused toward the enemy area.

Implementation Notes

  • Ice now has dedicated Max VFX replay and cast branches in CombatMaxVfxOverlay instead of relying on the generic status recipe.
  • Ice travel no longer uses the beam VFX. The medium and strong paths use moving ice_projectile, freeze, ice_shards, wind, snow, and pack-hit layers.
  • The separate mastery-beam entry point also routes Ice through the ice-block travel helper, so Ice should not fall back to a laser-like path.
  • Delayed impact lighting was added so Ice flashes land with the impact rather than glowing early during travel.
  • Strong Ice layers blizzard snow, wind, frost, shard rain, impact blocks, light, and camera kick while keeping the enemy area as the visual focus.

Debug Gallery

The existing VFX gallery remains the preferred place to compare Ice amount scaling.

  • res://scenes/debug/vfx_gallery_show.tscn can play Ice full sequence, travel, impact, and label phases.
  • Use amount presets 4, 12, and 50 to check ice ball, windy ice block, and blizzard behavior.
  • Labels are still handled above the Max VFX overlay by the combat presenter path.
