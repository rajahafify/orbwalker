Research Notes

The most useful direction from the references is that juice works when it makes input feel immediate and events feel legible. The counterpoint is just as useful for Orbwalker: puzzle combat can become unreadable if every tile, label, and portrait screams at once.

  • GDC Vault: Juice It or Lose It frames juice as small tricks that make interaction more satisfying, including particles and staged excess.
  • Game Developer: Don't Juice It or Lose It is the caution flag: context and immersion matter, not just more eye candy.
  • Designing Game Feel: A Survey describes juicing as amplified polish that can improve empowerment and clarity of feedback.
  • Game Feel in Pygame summarizes practical screenshake, particle, and squash/stretch patterns that map well to lightweight Godot implementation.

P1BoardLow Art

When a valid path releases, selected orbs hold for a split beat, brighten, then pop in sequence toward the match center.

  • Juice gain: makes the player feel the match was recognized before the board mutates.
  • Godot shape: resolve-presenter pre-clear tween with 40-70 ms stagger and reduced-motion instant fallback.
P1BoardMotion

Each orb type lands with a tiny personality: fire snaps hot, ice glides, earth thuds, gold bounces lightly.

  • Juice gain: refill stops feeling like a mechanical grid update.
  • Keep it readable by applying personality to easing and squash only, not changing final cell timing.
P1CombatAudio

Layer short synthetic transients so fire crackles, ice pings, earth knocks, armor clanks, and player damage has a duller body hit.

  • Juice gain: players can identify impact type even when watching the board rather than the enemy portrait.
  • Implementation note: keep duration under 260 ms and use pitch variation by damage amount or combo tier.
P1CombatNo New Asset

On damage, draw a colored ring at the hit point, then let the damage label burst from that same center.

  • Juice gain: ties number, element, and enemy HP loss into one visual sentence.
  • Balance guard: one ring per resolved element, not per individual particle.
P2BoardCombat

Let each cascade step advance a rhythmic pitch, label scale, and mastery meter pulse. The player hears and sees the combo count climbing.

  • Juice gain: cascades become a rising payoff instead of repeated clears.
  • Risk: cap pitch and label scale after combo 4 so late cascades do not become shrill.
P2CombatVFX

When a stream reaches mastery, the card mouth glows, briefly overfills, then settles back to the true progress value.

  • Juice gain: makes meter gain feel physical and earned.
  • Godot shape: tween visual fill to value plus 3-5 percent overshoot, then settle to actual value.
P2UICharacter

Enemy intent icons subtly track the board cursor or selected path when they are relevant to the upcoming damage/block decision.

  • Juice gain: the enemy feels reactive without changing mechanics.
  • Risk: only enable during player input; disable during resolve to avoid distraction.
P2UIAudio

Buying an item should make the card dip into the loadout/HUD, flash the affected stat, and play a short register-specific sound.

  • Juice gain: purchases feel like power entering the run, not a row disappearing.
  • Use restrained effects here; shop clarity matters more than spectacle.
P3CombatCharacter

If a hit drops the enemy below a threshold but does not kill, add a stagger pose, brief portrait desaturation, and a lower idle intensity.

  • Juice gain: health changes become embodied, which improves combat readability.
  • Risk: needs careful enemy-art handling so it does not imply a mechanical stun.
